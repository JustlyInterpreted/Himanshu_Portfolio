1. Company Background & Business Model

1.1 Company Overview

Zomato Ltd. is India’s leading online food delivery platform, operating in over 500 cities, connecting customers, restaurants, and delivery partners via a proprietary technology platform.

Key Business Segments:

  • Food Delivery: Core B2C platform
  • Dining Out: Restaurant discovery and reservations
  • Hyperpure: B2B supply chain for restaurants
  • Zomato Pro: Customer loyalty program

1.2 Promoter & Management Structure

Promoter Details

  • No identifiable promoter (professionally managed)
  • Key Shareholders:
  • Deepinder Goyal (CEO): 5.55%
  • Info Edge (India) Ltd.: 18.68%
  • ESOP Pool: ~10%

Board Composition

  • Independent Directors: 4/8 (50%)
  • Woman Director: Present
  • Audit Committee: Majority independent, SEBI compliant

1.3 Competitive Landscape

  • Primary Competitor: Swiggy (unlisted)
  • Market Share: ~50% in food delivery (duopoly)
  • Competitive Advantages: Early entry (2010), strong brand, robust technology

Insight: Zomato’s market leadership and technology infrastructure provide a strong competitive edge in a duopoly market.

11. Conclusion

Zomato’s IPO is a well-structured offering, fully compliant with SEBI ICDR Regulations 2018. The company demonstrates strong market leadership, a scalable technology platform, and a clear path to profitability (FY2024 EBITDA: ₹186 Cr, PAT: ₹351 Cr). However, the IPO’s premium valuation (P/S 29.9x) raises overvaluation concerns.

Key Conclusions:

  • Regulatory Compliance: Exemplary adherence
  • Pricing: Overvalued but justified by growth
  • Risk-Reward: High growth with risks
  • Market Impact: Boosts fintech/foodtech IPO sentiment
