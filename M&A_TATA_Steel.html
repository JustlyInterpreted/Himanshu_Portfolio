M&A Deal Analysis

Tata Steel's Acquisition of Bhushan Steel

Executive Summary

Acquirer: Tata Steel Ltd.

Target: Bhushan Steel Ltd. (under NCLT)

Transaction Value: ₹35,200 Cr

Deal Type: Distressed Asset Acquisition (CIRP)

Completion Date: May 2018

Strategic Rationale: Capacity expansion, raw material security, market consolidation

Assessment: Strategically sound, creating significant value through synergies and consolidation

1. Transaction Structure & Deal Mechanics

1.1 Deal Overview

Process: NCLT resolution under IBC 2016

Bid Process: Competitive bidding against JSW Steel, Vedanta, others

Winning Bid: ₹35,200 Cr (72% creditor recovery)

Funding: Debt + equity combination

Approvals: CCI, NCLT, RBI, statutory clearances

Insight: Competitive bidding ensured fair valuation, maximizing creditor recovery.

2. Strategic Rationale & Deal Drivers

2.1 Strategic Objectives

Primary Drivers

  • Capacity Expansion: From 12.9 MTPA to 18.4 MTPA
  • Raw Material Security: Captive iron ore and coal mines
  • Market Consolidation: Strengthen market position
  • Operational Synergies: Leverage Tata Steel's expertise
  • Geographic Expansion: Stronger Eastern India presence

2.2 Market Context

Indian Steel Industry

Market Size: ~100 MTPA (2018)

Growth Rate: 7–8% CAGR expected

Key Players: Tata Steel, JSW Steel, SAIL, Jindal Steel

Policy: National Steel Policy 2017 (300 MTPA by 2030)

Competitive Landscape

Pre-acquisition: Tata Steel (13%), JSW Steel (12%), SAIL (16%)

Post-acquisition: Tata Steel #2 private player

Positioning: Enhanced scale and efficiency

2.3 Bhushan Steel Profile

Company Overview

Capacity: 5.5 MTPA crude steel

Products: Hot/cold rolled coils, galvanized sheets

Assets: Integrated plant at Dhenkanal, Odisha

Raw Materials: Captive iron ore, coal linkages

Financial Distress

Total Debt: ₹56,000 Cr

Default Amount: ₹47,000 Cr

Issues: Overleveraging, working capital, governance

Insight: The acquisition aligned with Tata Steel’s strategic goals, leveraging Bhushan’s assets to enhance market leadership.

Financial Risks

  • Debt Servicing: Increased leverage

Macroeconomic Risks

  • Economic Slowdown: Impacts steel demand
  • Interest Rates: Affects debt servicing
  • Currency Fluctuation: Impacts import costs
  • Government Policy: Industrial policy changes

6.3 Mitigation Strategies
